About Our Organization

The Right To Live In Peace was founded in 1995 by a group of concerned citizens who believed that peace is not just the absence of conflict, but the presence of justice, understanding, and mutual respect.

Our History

What started as a small community initiative has grown into a global movement. Our founders, inspired by the peaceful revolutions of the late 20th century, created this organization to:

  • Document and share stories of peaceful conflict resolution
  • Provide educational resources about non-violent communication
  • Connect peace advocates from around the world
  • Support grassroots peace-building initiatives

Our Values

We are guided by these core principles:

  1. Non-violence: We believe in resolving conflicts through peaceful means
  2. Dignity: Every human being deserves respect and dignity
  3. Justice: True peace requires justice for all
  4. Dialogue: Understanding comes through open, honest communication
  5. Community: Peace is built through strong, connected communities

Recognition

Our work has been recognized by:

  • United Nations Peace Prize (2001)
  • Nobel Peace Prize Nomination (2003, 2007)
  • International Peace Foundation Award (2005)
  • Community Service Excellence Award (2010)
